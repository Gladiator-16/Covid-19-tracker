COVID-19 virus epidemic definition:

The World Health Organization declared COVID-19 a pandemic because of the unusually fast rate in which the virus is spreading. The novel coronavirus has infected more than half a million people worldwide and is present in more than 175 countries. The difference between epidemic and pandemic is that An epidemic becomes a pandemic when it is spreaded globally. More information can be found here.

Mesurement steps to cope with COVID-19 virus:

  • Intelligence:
    1. a. Using search & detection means to detect infected surfaces, domain and beings (Human, animals, flora, etc.).
    2. b. Survey to understand the extent of the pandemic.
      • 1. How Germany is managing its coronavirus epidemic? How is that germany has relatively low mortality rate respect to the extent escalating infections? Good Health-system and availability involved with reasonable relationship (ratio) between elderly and younger (infected )population. Answer.
      • 2. How South Korea has Flattened its coronavirus Curve? Answer.
      • 3. What is the best data-base management method? Graph Databases & Unified system.
      • 4. What is the effect of the closure and the form of isolation due to the coronavirus pandemic? For example, Social, Education, Economically and etc.
      • 5. What are the most advanced treatments and means to cope with conavirus pandemic? vaccines & drugs and advanced technology.
      • 6. List of FAQs on Diagnostic Testing for SARS-CoV-2s and example to rapid serology test to Detect Exposure to COVID-19.
    3. c. Monitor the patients and the infected casualties by approved method.
  • Research:
    • a. Analysis of the virus structure and its spread including comparison to former viral epidemics
    • b. Discovering the virus source
    • c. Mutation mechanism process
  • Healing treatment:
  • a. Prevention through isolation, use of masks and protective equipment (clothing, shoes, etc.).
  • b. Healing through inhibitory drugs at various stages.
  • c. Dealing with the disease - respirators, being in isolation, (preventing infection and exacerbation).
  • d. Total healing - Understanding how the virus works and using antibodies or similar drugs to deal with.

  • a. The psychological effects of isolation.
  • b. Failure of drug trials.
  • c. Loads in the emergency rooms.
  • d. Overload in shops & department stores.
  • e. Economic activity reduction and unemployment.
    • Actions:
  • a. Concurrent coping - learn about how it spreads, sticks, and acts while trying to prevent its spread and delay.
  • b. Use of similar viruses medications and antibodies from people who have healed.
